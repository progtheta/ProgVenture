Chapter 1: Introduction to Variables

What are variables?

Variables are like containers in programming that can store different types of information. They allow us to store and work with data in our programs.

For example, let's say you love movies. We can use a variable to store your favorite movie title:

// Declare and initialize a variable named "favoriteMovie"
char favoriteMovie[50] = "Spider-Man: Homecoming";

This creates a variable named "favoriteMovie" and assigns it the value "Spider-Man: Homecoming". Now, whenever you want to refer to your favorite movie, you can use the variable instead of typing the title repeatedly.

Variables in C can store different types of information, such as numbers, characters, and even words or sentences like movie titles.
